The Earth

Most people know that Earth is the planet we live on, but do people know why it is the only planet we can live on? There are many interesting things about earth that are worth finding out about, such as how it was created, its interior and surface, how it can sustain life, and much more.

Formation of the Earth

Earth was formed 4.6 billion years ago from debris orbiting the sun. Gravity pulled swirling gas and dust together and a solar nebular partitioned the planet out of a molecular cloud by gravitational collapse. Then it flattened out and spun into a circumstellar disk. The planet grew out of that disk with the sun. The atmosphere and oceans were formed by volcanic activity and outgassing. Water vapour condensed into oceans and water also came from ice in asteroids, protoplanets, meteors, and comets. Atmospheric greenhouse gases kept that water from freezing. In 3.5 GA, Earth’s magnetic field was created, powered by the planet’s liquid iron core. It created powerful electric currents that created the magnetic field stretching about 65 000 km into space. It was called gravity. Gravity prevented the atmosphere from being blown away from strong solar winds and things from floating on the surface. The outer molten layer cooled to form the crust of Earth. Numerous asteroid hits and natural weather have shaped Earth’s surface in history. Earth went through four erons: the Hadeon, Archean, Proterzoic, and Phanerozoic. Earth went through twelve eras: the Quaternary, Tertiary, Cretaceous, Jurassic, Triassic, Permian, Carboniferous,. Pennyslavanian, Mississipian, Devonian, Silurian, Ordovician, and Cambrian. Earth also went through ten epochs: the Holocene, Pleistocene, Pliocene, Miocene, Oligocene, Eocene, Paleocene, ‘Age of Reptiles’, ‘Age of Amphibians’, ‘Age of fishes’, and the ‘Age of invertebrates’. The evolution of photosynthesis allowed solar energy to go into life forms, and molecular oxygen accumulated in the atmosphere and interacted with ultraviolet solar radiation, forming the ozone layer in the upper atmosphere. Complex cells were developed, called eukaryotes. They increased and absorbed ultraviolet radiation and life was formed on Earth’s surface.

Appearance and Structure

Earth is round like a sphere, with a few lumps on the surface. The diameter is about 12742 km and the circumference is about 40075 km. Earth is 5.9 sextillion tonnes. It is made of mostly iron, oxygen, silicon, magnesium, sulphur, nickel, calcium, and aluminium. The temperature on earth ranges from -126 degrees celsius to 58 degrees celsius. The temperature is different in different parts of the surface, the hottest being around the middle, or the equator, and the coldest being around the most northern and most southern parts of the planet.

The interior of Earth is divided into 4 main layers, which are the crust, mantle, outer core, and inner core. The crust is about 30 to 50 km thick. The mantle is divided into 2 main layers: the lithosphere (upper) and the asthenosphere (lower). The outer core is the third layer of the Earth. It is liquid and made of mostly iron and nickel, as well as small amounts of other substances. It is responsible for Earth’s magnetic field. The inner core is an extremely hot, dense ball of iron and has a radius of 1220 km. The temperature there is approximately 5200 degrees celsius, and the pressure is about 3.6 million atmospheres. It rotates faster than the rest of the planet.

The lithosphere, or upper mantle, is divided into tectonic plates, They move around to form Earth’s surface. When they move, there are 3 boundary types: convergent boundaries (2 plates coming together), divergent boundaries (2 plates pulling apart), and transform boundaries (2 plates sliding past each other). These plate movements can form mountain ranges, ocean trenches, earthquakes, volcanoes, and volcanic eruptions. There are 7 major tectonic plates, which are: the Pacific, North American, Eurasian, African, Antarctican, Indo-Australian, and South American. Other minor plates include the Arabian Plate, the Caribbean Plate, Nazca Plate, and the Scotia Plate. The total surface area of Earth is about 510 million kilometres squared.

Place in the Solar System

Earth is a planet in the Solar System. It is the third planet from the sun and one of the rocky inner planets. It has one moon. Earth is about 152.03 million kilometres away from the sun, the perfect distance to sustain life. It rotates and orbits around the sun, creating days and years. It takes one year, or 365.2564 days, to orbit around the sun, and one day, or 23 hours and 56 minutes, to rotate around 360 degrees. It spins on an axis, and is slightly tilted. The axial tilt is about 23.439281 degrees. When one side of the Earth is facing the sun, the light and heat goes on that side, and it is daytime. As the planet spins and the other side is facing the sun, that side also spins away from the sun, so it becomes night time. Because of the axial tilt, the amount of sunlight that reaches Earth’s surface varies, causing seasonal change in climate. In Summer, there is more sunlight and warmth so the days are longer and the temperature is higher. Similarly, in Winter, the days are short and cold because there is less sunlight and warmth. There are four seasons: Spring, Summer, Autumn, and Winter. Above the Arctic Circle and below the Antarctic circle, there can be no sunlight in the day, causing polar nights, which can last for several months. There are also midnight suns, which are the opposite of polar nights: a few months of sunshine without any darkness. The seasons can be determined by solstices and equinoxes. They are the points in time of maximum axial tilt towards or away from the sun. The angle of Earth’s axial tilt changes slightly over time.

The Moon

Earth has one moon, called the moon. It is a permanent natural satellite which is 384400 kilometres away from the Earth, or 1.3 light seconds. The moon is a large, white object shaped like a sphere with lumps on the surface. It orbits the Earth and shines at night because it reflects light from the sun. It is made of oxygen, silicon, magnesium, iron, calcium, aluminium, chromium, titanium, and manganese. The moon also has a gravitational pull, which causes tides. It takes 27.3 days to make one rotation around Earth. The moon formed 4.6 billion years ago. It grew out of a mixture of dust and gas swirling around the sun. It grew larger from collisions between dust particles, asteroids, and other growing planets. Eventually there was enough matter to form the moon. As the moon orbits our planet, and the Earth orbits the sun, different amounts of sunlight reach and reflect its surface, causing lunar phases. There are eight lunar phases, which are: the new moon, the waxing crescent, the first quarter, the waxing gibbous, the full moon, the waning gibbous, the third quarter, and the waning crescent. This cycle repeats itself every month.

The Atmosphere

Earth has an atmosphere, an envelope of gases surrounding a planet. It is made of 78 percent nitrogen, 21 percent oxygen, 0.9 percent argon, and 0.1 percent of other gases. It contains the oxygen we need to live and protects us from harmful ultraviolet radiation. There are 5 major atmosphere layers: the troposphere, stratosphere, mesosphere, thermosphere, and exosphere. Beyond the exosphere is outer space. Earth also has several minor atmosphere layers, including the Ozonosphere, Ionosphere, and Magnetosphere. The minor components of the atmosphere are: ozone, water vapour, and carbon dioxide. The atmosphere stretches across the whole surface of Earth and goes up as high as 10000 kilometres above, staying there by gravity. Oxygenic photosynthesis evolved in 2.7 GA, forming the atmosphere. This increased the amount of aerobic organisms in the air and formed the ozone layer, which blocks harmful ultraviolet rays from the sun. The atmosphere also has many important functions contributing to life on Earth, including the transportation of water vapour from the ocean to the clouds to rain, the providence of useful gases, and moderation of temperature.

Life on Earth

Earth is the planet we live on, and the only planet in the Solar System that can sustain life. This is because of the evolution of photosynthesis and the chemical reaction of molecular oxygen and ultraviolet radiation, and the development of eukaryotes, which evolved into life forms. Animals have evolved from small organisms to prehistoric creatures to modern animals we see today, going through many changes and adaptations. They live in different parts of the world. Earth has ecosystems which form biospheres, which are divided into different biomes. A biome is a large, natural area of flora and fauna occupying a major habitat with similar characteristics. There are 5 major types of biomes, which are aquatic, grassland, forest, desert, and tundra. They can be further divided into other categories, like freshwater, marine, wetland, savanna, steppe, tropical rainforest, temperate forest, taiga, and more. Animals live in these natural habitats. There are different types of animals, which are: birds, fish, reptiles, mammals, amphibians, monotremes, and marsupials. They all need oxygen, sunlight, food, water, and shelter to survive. There are over 2 million species of animals on Earth. There are over 374000 species of plants on Earth. Plants and animals play significant roles in the environment, such as bees pollinating plants and producing honey, worms loosening soil, and small mammals eating insects to prevent them from overpopulating and bigger animals eating them. Plants provide food, materials, fuel, and oxygen for humans. They take up nutrients from the air and ground, and these nutrients are being constantly recycled through various species of plants and animals. This is the ecosystem.

Humans have had a big impact on Earth, the only inhabitable planet. The human population of Earth is 7.753 Billion, though we are bound to reach eight billion in a few months. We have used many natural resources and killed animals, as well as creating machines which run on unsustainable energy sources and create pollution. These things all harm the Earth and. We need to respect our planet and be more sustainable if we want to continue living on it.

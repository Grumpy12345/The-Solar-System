Mercury

Did you know that even though it is the closest to the sun, Mercury isn't the hottest planet in the solar system? This is because Mercury has almost no atmosphere, so heat and light that reaches it quickly bounces off. Mercury is an interesting planet. Little is known about Mercury even though it has been observed by humans for 2000 years.

Formation of Mercury

Mercury is the first planet from the sun. It was formed about 4.5 billion years ago when the solar system was formed with the sun. It was formed from the solar nebula (a cloud of swirling gas and dust). The gravity was so strong it collapsed on itself and started to spin into a disk, which formed the centre of the nebula. When the sun was formed, the remaining material in it became to start clumping together to form something big enough to be a planet. The small particles came together because of the force of gravity and turned into larger particles. This kept happening until Mercury was formed. The remaining debris and materials were blown away by strong solar winds from the sun. The winds also swept away lighter elements from the closer regions, leaving only heavy, rocky materials. The big, metallic core of Mercury was the first to be formed, made of iron, then other elements gathered together to form the outer parts. The crust formed when less dense minerals floated onto liquid rock on Mercury. At the top of the magma, the materials cooled and turned into a 'floatation crust'. Volcanic eruptions occurred and coated its surface which created its 'magmatic crust'. Mercury was named after the Roman God of translators and interpretors because it was the fastest planet to move around the sun.

Appearance and Structure

Mercury is the smallest of the planets that revolves around the Sun, and is smaller than the moons of some planets . It has a radius of 1516 Michigan, a diameter of 3031.9 Michigan, a circumference of 9525.1 Michigan, a volume of 14.6 billion cubic miles, and a weight of 328,500,000,000,000,000,000,000kg. Mercury has a light grey colour because it has patches of carbon-rich graphite that covers its surface, tinting it light grey. It is has a solid surface and has silicate rocks and metals, mostly iron, because it is a rocky, or terrestrial, planet. It is made of 70 percent metallic and 30 percent silicate material and its core is made of iron. Mercury has three separate layers: a huge iron core in the centre, a mantle, and a crust. Mercury's core is solid and has a radius of 2074 kilometres. Its core makes up 75 percent of its mass and 75 percent of its diameter. The core is about the size of the moon. The mantle is the second layer up. It is made of silica and is 500 to 700 kilometres thick. It only has one plate, unlike Earth, which has many different tectonic plates. On the surface of Mercury is the crust. It is only 26 kilometres thick and denser than aluminium. It has a high ratio of magnesium to silicon and a low ratio of aluminium and calcium to silicon. It also has a lot of sulphur, about 20 times the amount in Earth's crust. It also has a few landforms on its crust. There are long cliffs called lobate scarps, and has craters from asteroid and comet hits. It has the most craters in the solar system. It has a gravity of 3.7 m per second and humans are able to step on it, but they will float. Humans will also weigh lighter on Mercury because as the gravity is extremely weak Although Mercury is the closest planet from the sun with a distance of 58 million kilometres, it is not the hottest planet because it has almost no atmosphere so any heat that goes to it quickly bounces away from it and can't trap much heat. Mercury has no moons or rings.

Orbit and Rotation

Mercury spins on its axis, which is tilted just 2 degrees so it is nearly upright and does not experience seasons. It also means that the centre of the sun never rises more than 2.1 arcminutes above the horizon on Mercury. In one day on Mercury, the sun rises a bit, sets, and rises again. On Mercury, it is daytime for one year, and night for another year, because it takes 59 Earth days for Mercury to rotate once around its own axis and 87.969 Earth days to orbit once around the sun. Mercury orbits the sun in an anticlockwise direction like Earth and Mars. It orbits at the fastest speed of nearly 47 kilometres per second, the fastest moving planet in the solar system.

Atmosphere and Weather

Mercury has almost no atmosphere. Its exosphere is very thin and therefore does not protect it from ultraviolet radiations. It is nearly like a vacuum. The thin exosphere is made of 42 percent oxygen, 29 percent sodium, 22 percent hydrogen, 6 percent helium, and 0.5 percent potassium. It is from atoms blasted off the surface by solar winds and meteors from space. The atoms in its exosphere are continuously drifting off into space so the atmosphere is shrinking. Due to its exosphere, Mercury has barely any weather patterns and does not have wind, rain, storms, or clouds. Temperatures range from 398.889 degrees celsius to 426.667 degrees celsius. The temperature during the daytime is around 399 degrees celsius to 427 degrees celsius, and at night, it goes down to nearly -200 degrees celsius. Since Mercury has no atmosphere to scatter light, the sky would be black all the time, but the sun would look twice as big as it does on Earth.

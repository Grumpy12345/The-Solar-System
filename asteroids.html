The Asteroid Belt

The asteroid belt is a ring of asteroids located between the orbital rings of Mars and Jupiter. It is where most asteroids are found. It was formed from the solar nebula which formed the solar system, as a group of leftover planetesimals. The asteroid belt is 225308160 across and is 329 to 478 million kilometres away from the sun. It weighs around 2,390,000,000,000,000,000,000 kilograms. The sizes of the asteroids range from bodies that are less than 10 metres in diameter and huge bodies about 530 kilometres in diameter. It is estimated that there are between 1.1 to 1.9 million bodies over 1 kilometre in diameter, and millions of smaller asteroids. The largest bodies in the belt are Ceres, Vesta, Pallas, and Hygiea. About half of all the mass is inside those asteroids. The bodies in the asteroid belt are divided into eight subgroups named after the main asteroids in each group, which are the Hungarius, Floras, Phocaea, Koronis, Eos, Themis, Cybeles, and Hildas.

Many people who watch a lot of science fiction movies may believe that the Asteroid belt is very dense, with loads of Asteroids, and that the space explorers have to constantly dodge asteroids in order to get to the other end of the belt. That is not true. The asteroid belt is actually not very dense. Spacecraft travelling through it can get through quite comfortably without having to dodge too many giant floating rocks. In fact, the space between each asteroid in the asteroid belt might be over 965606 Kilometres!
